


Look into any chef’s kitchen and you’ll see stacks and stacks of simple steel skillets. The go-to utensils for finishing many foods, they are not as common in the home cook’s arsenal, but they should be. Mark Henry, an Australian engineer, has upgraded them with improvements like an openwork handle that stays cool and one-piece construction without rivets so food residue doesn’t get trapped. He has applied similar features to cast-iron skillets. Both come preseasoned: Solidteknics Aus-Ion steel skillets, $79.95 to $189.99 at JB Prince, jbprince.com; AUSfonte cast-iron skillets, $99.99 to $129.95 at amazon.com.
