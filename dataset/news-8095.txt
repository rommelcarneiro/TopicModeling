


CHESTER, Pa. — Chris Pontius and C. J. Sapong scored in the first half, and the Philadelphia Union beat New York City F.C., 2-0, on Saturday for its team-record fifth straight home victory.

Pontius opened the scoring in the 26th minute when he finished a short cross from Tranquillo Barnetta with a right-footed shot from the top of the 6-yard box.

Sapong got his goal in the 41st minute, redirecting a right-footed blast from Sebastien Le Toux with a well-placed flick into the goal’s left corner.

The Union improved to 4-3-0. N.Y.C.F.C. dropped to 1-2-4.
