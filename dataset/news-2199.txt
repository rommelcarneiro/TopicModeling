


When the Cure last played New York in 2011, the show was dedicated to the band’s restless first three albums, before it found its way as an innovator of stadium-scale mope. The group returns this week for three shows at Madison Square Garden — part of a lengthy American tour — with a broader, career-spanning mandate.

That means the familiar blend of morbidity and ecstatic release, songs that begin near post-punk and end up somewhere far more considered and theatrical, and the trademark Robert Smith vocals, which somehow telegraph both insular reckoning and utter lack of interest all at once. (thegarden.com.)
